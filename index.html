Bestiary & Lore

Every creature you will meet, under its proper name, with behaviour, how to survive it, and a spoiler-light lore note. Nothing here reveals story twists, character fates or endings; it only names what stalks you and hints at where each thing fits in the island's history, so you can draw your own conclusions in play.

These are collectively known as the Reanimals: the island's former inhabitants, human and animal, returned wrong. Some still go through the motions of the lives they had. Watching what each one is trying to do is half the lore of this game.

Common enemies

Critters

Chapters 2, 3, 4, 6 and 8

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourSmall, skittish creatures hiding under masks and household objects. They never attack; they bolt the moment they are seen and hole up somewhere nearby.

How to handleChase them down and put them out of their misery: five of them carry Masks you need for 100%. The tracker tab flags every one.

Lore noteWhy would something so small and harmless hide its face? Look closely at what each one has chosen to wear.

Skins

Chapters 1 and 2 mainly

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourThe skin-crawling things: flattened, emptied husks that slither along floors and up walls like snakes, drawn to anything living. They are tended to, almost lovingly, by the Sniffer.

How to handleThey cannot jump gaps and cannot open doors, so both stop them cold, though walls only delay them. Keep moving, put obstacles between you, and never linger in a dead end.

Lore noteConsider what a Skin is missing, and then notice what the Sniffer spends his working day doing. The game never says it aloud; it does not need to.

Boomers

Chapter 3 and other flooded areas

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourBloated, waterlogged humanoids that lie utterly still among the island's genuine corpses, gorged on drowned food and drink. Get close and they lurch upright, close in, and detonate.

How to handleFrom the boat, a harpoon pops them safely at range. On foot, watch any body that seems a little too intact, and if one rises, run; distance is the only defence.

Lore noteThey are indistinguishable from the dead because that is exactly what they are. The flood fed them; think about what that implies about how long they have been down there.

Seagulls

Chapters 3 and 6, beaches and cliffs

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourSwarming shore birds that mob you in numbers on open sand, and they answer to something much larger.

How to handleSteady crowbar swings knock them out of the air. Fight with your back to a wall so they cannot circle behind you.

Lore noteOrdinary animals on this island are never quite ordinary. Note who they gather around, and who they flee from.

Spider Kids

Chapters 4 and 5, the orphanage

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourSmall, skittering child-like figures that attack in waves, some with spears. Individually feeble, genuinely dangerous in a pack, and they clearly answer to someone.

How to handleNever let them surround you. Fight near doorways and corners to funnel them, and in the late arena fight, their thrown spears are your ammunition; pick them up and throw them back.

Lore noteLook at where they live, what they wear and what they have built. The clock tower was something before it was this, and they did not choose it.

Soldiers

Chapters 7 and 8, the trenches and hospital

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourThe war dead, still carrying out their final orders: patrolling, manning guns, clutching grenades. Some are threats; several are scripted vignettes that simply play out as you pass.

How to handleWatch before you act. Shotgun carriers and machine gunners must be avoided or outrun; grenade men must be given distance. Many are better witnessed than fought, and the vignettes carry most of the war's story.

Lore noteA war was fought on this island, and for these men it never ended. Their uniforms, their positions and their last acts sketch out which side was doing what, and to whom.

Town People

Chapter 2, the village

BehaviourThe husks of the town's former residents, drifting around the streets and buildings. Notably, they are docile towards the Sniffer, and he towards them.

How to handleMostly a stealth consideration rather than a combat one; treat them as eyes and ears for the thing that keeps them.

Lore noteA whole town of people who never left, and one caretaker still keeping order among them. Ask yourself what routine he is preserving, and for whom.

No verified screenshot available for this one; you will know them when you see them standing still in the village.

The bosses, properly named

Sniffer

Chapters 1 and 2

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourA tall, suited humanoid with a grotesquely oversized head, near-blind and hunting entirely by sound. When he detects you the politeness vanishes: he drops to all fours and runs you down like an animal. He is always busy, dragging, sorting, ironing, endlessly working.

How to handleSilence and hiding spots. Move with ambient noise, use briefcases, trolleys and dryers, and throw objects to pull him away. His full fight strategy is in the Tracker tab.

Lore noteHe drives an ice cream truck, an object built purely to make children feel safe. Everything about him is a routine of care curdled into something else. Watch his chores when he has not seen you; they explain the Skins better than any note could.

The Pelican

Chapter 3

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourA gigantic, contorted pelican with a horribly animate pouch, commanding the seagull flocks and nesting at the top of the lighthouse. It snatches things, and people, and keeps them.

How to handleYou cannot fight it, only outrun it. When the chase begins, sprint and do not stop for anything; being caught is instant death.

Lore noteReal pelicans carry their catch home in that pouch. Consider what a creature of this island might choose to collect, and why a lighthouse, a building made to guide the lost to safety, is its nest.

The Mother

Chapters 4 and 5

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourThe enormous spider-like thing dwelling beneath the orphanage, and the reason the Spider Kids behave like a brood. They shelter under her, and she guards them with genuine ferocity.

How to handleStealth first, flight second, and only at the very end a real fight, using the kids' own spears. Her two emergence points and the safe positions for each are in the boss section of the Tracker tab.

Lore noteThe name is the lore. Something took on the one role those children were missing, in its own dreadful way. Whether that is monstrous or almost tender is left entirely to you.

The Brook Horse

Chapters 3 and 6, the open water

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourA towering figure with a human-like torso, long lank arms and a horse's skull for a head, standing silent in the flooded ruins. It seems merely curious from a distance; approach and it slips beneath the surface, and then your boat is simply gone. It is also supernaturally hard to put down.

How to handleIt guards its territory near the warship route, and you CAN blunder into it as early as Chapter 3 if you sail the wrong way, so treat distant silhouettes in the water with respect. It cannot be fought on the surface without artillery. The full encounter strategy is in the Tracker tab.

Lore noteThe Bäckahästen of Swedish folklore is a beautiful white horse that appears by the water and invites children onto its back, then walks into the depths and drowns them. Tarsier being a Swedish studio, this is that story with the beauty stripped away. It never needed to be lovely; children came to the water anyway.

The Sniper

Chapter 8

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourAn unseen marksman holed up in the clock tower, executing anything that steps into the light, including his own. You never properly see him; you see what he does.

How to handleLight is the enemy. Hug darkness, move between cover, and remember that he sees through any lit opening. The garage sequence and his abrupt end are covered in the Tracker tab.

Lore noteOf everything on the island he is the most human, no mutation required, just a man, a rifle and a doctrine. The game placing him among the monsters, and letting him shoot his own side, is its bleakest comment on the war.

The Sheep Beast

Chapters 7 to 9

Screenshots via the QuestDuo and PowerPyx walkthroughs. Tap to open full size.

BehaviourThe colossal sheep, sometimes called the Lamb, that begins shadowing you in the late game and does not stop. It is less an animal than a presence: wherever you go, it is already looming.

How to handleStealth and flight until the finale, where the game finally gives you the means to answer it. Its three-phase fight is in the Tracker tab.

Lore noteDeliberately the least said here, because this one is the game's story to tell. A lamb is the oldest symbol of innocence and sacrifice there is; keep that in mind, notice when and where it appears, and let the ending do the rest.
